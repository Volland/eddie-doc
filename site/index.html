What it does

Extract, map, review, and act — without ever rewriting your prose automatically.

◈ Extracts

Highlights, strikethroughs, underlines, sticky-note comments and caret/insert marks from the PDF via pdfjs — including the comment body and author.

⇄ Maps

Fuzzy text matching that strips AsciiDoc syntax first, so a highlighted phrase lines up with your markup-laden source. Unicode-aware (incl. Cyrillic).

▤ Shows

An activity-bar tree grouped Open / Needs review / Unmatched / Resolved, inline decorations with hovers, and Problems-panel diagnostics.

✎ Acts

Lightbulb quick-fixes: delete struck text character-precisely, replace a marked range, insert at a caret offset, or pull a remark in as a comment. All explicit and undoable.

☰ Triages

Clear a pile of unmatched marks in one keyboard pass, each with a pre-ranked shortlist of candidate source lines to link or skip.

⤓ Tracks

Resolution state and manual re-links persist in a diffable sidecar, <file>.review.json, next to your .adoc — preserved across re-maps.

How mapping works

Robust to the source and PDF not being character-identical.

  1. PDF markup annotations carry quadPoints — per-line rectangles over the marked text. Eddie Doc intersects those with the positioned text of each page to recover the words under the mark.
  2. Both the recovered text and every source line are normalized — AsciiDoc delimiters, roles, macros and inline markers stripped, lowercased, punctuation removed.
  3. They're compared with a blended Sørensen–Dice (bigram) + token-overlap score over a sliding window of source lines. The best span above the threshold wins.
  4. Low-confidence matches surface as Unmatched for a one-click manual re-link, rather than guessing.

The workflow

Navigate + review, edit by hand. Eddie Doc points you at the right line and remembers what you've handled.

  1. Open the .adoc you're reviewing and run Eddie Doc: Open PDF Review for AsciiDoc. Pick the annotated PDF.
  2. Annotations appear in the sidebar and as inline markers. Click to jump; hover a line to read the comment and mark it resolved.
  3. Edit your .adoc, then run Re-map Annotations to re-check positions. Resolution state is preserved across re-maps.

An open, portable artifact

The review lives in a committable, tool-agnostic sidecar — not locked inside the editor.

Every review is stored as <file>.review.json in the Eddie Doc Review Format: a versioned, schema-validated standard with relative paths and content hashes, so it travels with your repo and can be read by a CLI, a CI gate, or another editor.
